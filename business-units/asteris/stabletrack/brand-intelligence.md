## 1. Identity

StableTrack is an equine practice management platform built by Asteris, the market-leading veterinary PACS company (20+ years in veterinary imaging). It combines scheduling, documentation, billing, imaging, inventory, and payments in one workflow.

**Status:** Live. Launched at AAEP 2025. Now in broader rollout.
**Website:** stabletrack.ai
**Parent:** Asteris (asteris.com) — Keystone Omni PACS, DICOM-compliant, trusted across equine and companion animal practices globally.

**One-line positioning:** The first AI-native practice management system built specifically for equine veterinarians working in the field.

**Primary angle:** Revenue recovery, not efficiency. StableTrack makes sure you get paid for the work you already do.

**Target segments:**
1. Solo / Independent Equine Veterinarians
2. Growing Multi-Vet Equine Practices (2–4+ vets)
3. Equine Hospitals & Multi-Location Groups

## 2. Values

Three values, ranked by priority. When they conflict, the higher-ranked value wins.

**1. Reliability.** StableTrack works. In the barn. On the road. Without internet. At 6am and at 9pm. This extends to the company: support answers, updates don't break workflows, promises get kept. For an audience burned by software before, reliability is the price of entry.

**2. Practicality.** Every feature must pass one test: does this make a vet's next farm call easier? The interface works on a phone in sunlight. Voice-to-SOAP means no typing. Invoices generate from the exam record. If a feature requires a tutorial, it's too complex.

**3. Exceptional assistance.** The product assists — AI captures notes, auto-generates invoices, tracks inventory. The support team assists — real people who understand equine practice. Framed as "assistance," not "automation" or "intelligence." The AI earns its place by saving time quietly, not by asserting authority.

## 3. AI trust framework

49% of AAEP members are over 50. They are not opposed to technology — they use digital X-ray, ultrasound, cloud storage. But they are skeptical of technology that overclaims or tries to replace their judgment.

**Rule:** Lead with the outcome, not the technology. The word "AI" should almost never lead a headline, subject line, or opening sentence.

| Instead of | Write |
|-----------|-------|
| "AI-powered documentation" | "Talk through the exam. Your notes write themselves." |
| "Intelligent billing automation" | "Finish the exam, and the invoice is already done." |
| "Machine learning optimizes your workflow" | "Less time on paperwork. More time with horses." |
| "Our AI assistant helps you..." | "StableTrack handles the admin so you don't have to." |

**Status quo argument:** The strongest conversion lever for skeptical buyers is the quantified cost of inaction — not a feature pitch. Invoices entered one day late are worth 15% less. Delayed more than a day, 36% less (JAVMA 2024). Practices lose 5–10% of all charges. The risk isn't trying something new. The risk is continuing what you're doing.

## 4. Voice & tone

### 4.1 The mobile-first reading reality

The audience reads on their phone, between farm calls, in a truck, with dirty hands. 49% are over 50. They scan — they don't deep-read. You get 2–3 seconds on a subject line, 5–8 seconds on an opening paragraph.

**Rules for all content:**
- **Front-load the value.** Most important claim in sentence one.
- **One idea per screen.** A phone screen is ~4–5 short lines. Each screen-full = one complete thought.
- **Short paragraphs.** 1–3 sentences. No exceptions in customer-facing content.
- **No buried leads.** Never open with context, background, or "in today's fast-paced world." Open with the reader's problem or outcome.
- **Scannable structure.** Bold the key phrase per paragraph. If it works reading only the bold text, the structure is right.

### 4.2 Voice definition

**Practical authority with genuine empathy.** Speaks from inside equine practice, not about it.

- **Grounded.** Not "streamline your operations" but "stop doing paperwork at 10pm."
- **Honest.** If switching software takes effort, say so. Honesty is the fastest path to trust.
- **Clean arguments at the front.** Every piece opens with a single evaluable claim. No questions. No throat-clearing.

Strong openers: "The average equine practice loses 5–10% of revenue to missed charges." / "You did the work. StableTrack makes sure you get paid for it." / "StableTrack works without internet. Because barns don't have Wi-Fi."

Weak openers to avoid: "In today's rapidly evolving veterinary landscape..." / "Have you ever wondered..." / "At StableTrack, we believe..."

### 4.3 Language

**Use:** "In the field" / "At the barn" / "Capture every charge" / "Get paid for the work you already do" / "Works without internet" / "Less admin, more medicine" / "Purpose-built, not adapted" / "Your notes write themselves" / "The invoice is already done"

**Avoid:** "Revolutionize" / "Transform" / "Cutting-edge" / "Disrupt" / "Synergy" / "Leverage" / Generic SaaS language / AI-forward headlines / Opening questions / Throat-clearing intros

### 4.4 Tone by channel

| Channel | Register | Mobile note |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Cold email | Warm, peer-to-peer. Lead with pain point. | Subject <40 chars. First sentence = the pitch. <100 words total. |
| Website | Confident, benefit-led. Field language. | Headlines as standalone statements. |
| Blog | Educational, generous. Help, don't sell. | Bold takeaways. Subheads every 2–3 paragraphs. |
| Social | Conversational, field-aware. Pain before solution. | First line is the hook. |
| Demo/sales | Consultative. Demo workflows, not features. | One feature at a time for older audience. |
| Support | Patient, thorough. They're experts in their field. | Numbered steps. One action per step. Screenshots. |

### 4.5 Writing rules

- Short sentences. Active voice. Under 20 words per sentence when possible.
- 1–3 sentence paragraphs. White space is a feature.
- First sentence = clean argument. Specific claim, concrete number, or direct value statement.
- Clinical terms: correct (SOAP notes, ambulatory, lameness evaluation). Tech terms: plain (works without internet, not offline-first PWA).
- One CTA per piece. Low-commitment. "Book a 15-minute demo" not "Learn more."
- Body text never smaller than 16px / 12pt. High contrast always. No light gray on white.

## 5. Visual identity

### Logo
Stylized horse head line drawing above "StableTrack" wordmark (serif) with descriptor "EQUINE PRACTICE MANAGEMENT" in small caps. Warm brown/amber tone.
- Files: ST-logo-no_background.svg (transparent), ST-Brand_Board-1_0.png (reference)
- Clear space: height of horse icon on all sides
- Minimum size: descriptor text must remain legible

### Color palette

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| Equine Brown | #7e4925 | Primary. Headers, buttons, logo. |
| Saddle Amber | #C6761E | Secondary. CTAs, links, active states. |
| Saddle Brown | #8B4513 | Supporting. Borders, depth. |
| Sunset | #FF9507 | Accent. Alerts, badges. **Fails WCAG AA on white for small text.** Large headings/icons only. |
| Barn Cream | #fbfaf8 | Backgrounds. Warm off-white, not clinical. |

Avoid pure white backgrounds (use Barn Cream), pure black text (use Equine Brown or warm dark gray), cool blues/greens/grays.

### Typography

| Font | Role | Fallback stack |
|------|------|---------------|
| **Fraunces** (serif) | Display, headlines, hero text. Bold/semi-bold. Not below 18px. | Georgia, "Times New Roman", Times, serif |
| **Manrope** (sans-serif) | Body, UI, buttons, email. Regular 400, Medium 500, Semi-bold 600. Min 16px. | -apple-system, "Segoe UI", Roboto, "Helvetica Neue", Arial, sans-serif |

**Email:** Do not embed web fonts. Use fallback stacks only. Sans-serif for all email text. Georgia acceptable for a single hero heading. Inline all CSS. Min 16px body, 22px+ headlines.

### Imagery
- Vets with horses in real settings: barns, fields, trailers. Warm light, authentic, not staged.
- Technology used naturally (phone at barn, tablet during exam) — never as hero.
- No stock photography aesthetic. No clinical/sterile environments. No cool-toned corporate imagery.
- The vet and the horse are always the hero. Technology is in the background.

## 6. Narrative constraints

- Never claim revenue recovery numbers without citing the source (JAVMA 2024, AAHA, AVMA)
- Never position StableTrack as only for solo practices — website targets solo, multi-vet, and hospital
- Never disparage competitors by name — focus on category weaknesses ("tools built for clinics," "systems that require internet")
- Always ground claims in field reality
- Respect that these are highly educated professionals — never condescend
- AI framing: always "supports workflow and reduces admin" — never "replaces clinical judgment"
- Use the website's language: "Veterinarians stay fully in control."
