# StableTrack — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
> Reference file. Load for targeting, segmentation, email campaigns, persona-based content, or objection handling.

---

## Primary ICP
Independent equine ambulatory veterinarian

## Target segments

1. **Solo / Independent Equine Veterinarians** — core ICP. Highest pain, fastest sales cycle, most emotionally resonant messaging.
2. **Growing Multi-Vet Equine Practices (2–4+ vets)** — need coordination, standardization, visibility. Buying trigger: scaling complexity.
3. **Equine Hospitals & Multi-Location Groups** — need consistent workflows across field and clinic. Larger deal size, longer cycle.

Default to solo/independent for cold outreach and top-of-funnel. Address all three when context requires.

---

## Firmographics
- 1–4 veterinarians (52.8% of equine practices have 1–2 FTE vets)
- Mobile / ambulatory practice model
- Rural or semi-rural geography
- Limited or no dedicated admin support
- Revenue typically under $1.5M
- ~6 scheduled appointments/day (vs. 13–14 in companion animal)

## Demographics
- 65.7% female; 85% of vet students female
- 49% of AAEP members over age 50
- Associates in first 5 years = highest attrition risk
- 70.8% incurred vet school debt; 53.5% still paying
- Average: 5.3 days/week (busiest quarter), 13+ hours/day
- 104 on-call weeknights, 22 on-call weekends per year

---

## Daily workflow
- Travel between barns/farms (route-based, not appointment-based)
- Exams and treatments on-site, often alone without tech support
- Document findings manually or after hours
- Invoice after visits, often delayed hours or days
- Handle emergencies 24/7 (especially solo/two-vet)
- Manage inventory in truck/field kit
- Communicate with owners, trainers, barn managers (often different people per horse)

---

## Pain points (ranked)
1. **Revenue leakage** — 15–36% invoice decay from delayed entry (JAVMA 2024)
2. **After-hours admin** — documentation at night, primary burnout driver
3. **Connectivity** — no internet in barns, cloud tools fail
4. **Fragmented tools** — notes/billing/scheduling/imaging in separate systems
5. **Complex billing** — multi-owner horses, trainers vs. owners, split invoices
6. **Isolation** — solo practitioners bearing full operational load

---

## Buying triggers
- Realizing revenue is being lost (accountant, tax review, gut feeling)
- Frustration with current software (too limited, too complex, too clinic-centric)
- Practice growth increasing complexity
- Desire to reduce admin time / after-hours work
- Considering leaving equine practice — looking for sustainability
- Life event (new baby, health issue, associate leaving) forcing workflow reckoning

## Buying motivations
- Increase revenue without increasing workload
- One system, not five
- Reduce after-hours admin
- System that understands equine practice
- Get off legacy/underpowered software without painful migration
- Future-proof with AI-native tools

---

## Objection handling

| Objection | Response strategy |
|-----------|------------------|
| "Switching is painful" | Acknowledge. Emphasize migration support. Cost of staying (revenue loss, admin hours, burnout) vs. cost of switching. |
| "No time to learn new software" | Designed for field workflows. Demo voice-to-SOAP speed. Learning curve = hours, not weeks. |
| "Too expensive" | Frame against revenue recovered. Losing 5–10% of charges = StableTrack pays for itself in weeks. |
| "Current system works well enough" | Probe on after-hours docs, missed charges, billing delays. "Well enough" masks hidden costs. |
| "Burned by software before" | Built by Asteris — 20+ years. Not a startup. Free trial in the field. Already in use by equine practices. |

## Outbound segments (for campaigns)
1. **Reproduction & Breeding** — complex ownership, recurring visits, scheduling pain
2. **Performance & Sport Horse** — multi-owner syndicates, imaging volume, competition schedules
3. **Surgical & Hospital-Based** — complex billing, team workflows, higher procedure volume
4. **Solo / Mobile Practitioners** — admin burden, after-hours work, connectivity. Most emotionally resonant.
