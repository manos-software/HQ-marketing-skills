## Equine veterinary services market

- Global market ~$1.35B in 2024, projected $2.45B by 2032 (CAGR 6.5–7.5%)
- Broader equine healthcare (incl. pharma, diagnostics, supplements): $4.4B in 2025, projected $10.3B by 2035 (CAGR 11.2%)
- North America: 35–45% of global revenue. U.S. equine economy contributes $122B+ annually.
- U.S. equine population: 6.65–7.2 million horses
- ~3,785 equine-primary private veterinary clinics (AVMA 2022)
- Key drivers: equestrian sports growth, equine insurance, preventive care emphasis, digital transformation

## The workforce crisis

The equine veterinary profession is in structural decline. This is the most important context for StableTrack's positioning.

- Equine vets = 4.1% of all private practice vets in U.S. (~3,785 practitioners, AVMA 2022)
- 52.8% of equine practices have 1–2 FTE vets. Solo practitioners = 35–40% of AAEP membership.
- 49% of AAEP members are over age 50
- 65.7% of equine vets are female; 85% of vet students are female
- ~50% of new graduates leave equine practice within first 5 years
- New graduates entering equine practice: declined from 5.7% (2003) to ~1–1.4% (recent years)
- 4 in 10 associates actively considering leaving equine medicine (lifestyle/income)
- Equine practices open 13+ hours/day on weekdays on average
- Average: 104 on-call weeknights, 22 on-call weekends per year
- ~6 appointment slots/day in equine (vs. 13–14 in companion animal)

**Why they leave:** Lack of work-life balance, long hours, 24/7 emergency coverage, lower pay than companion animal, high student debt, administrative burden. 2025 Equine Veterinary Journal study confirmed unpredictable hours and heavy workloads as top dissatisfaction factors.

**What it means for StableTrack:** Revenue recovery + admin reduction = practice sustainability intervention. The vet who recovers $40K/year can hire relief help, take a weekend off, or avoid leaving equine practice entirely.

## Revenue leakage — quantified

Industry-wide:
- Average practice misses 5–10% of all charges (AVMA, AAHA, KSM, ezyVet)
- 17% of lab tests go unbilled (AAHA)
- Practice grossing $2M could leave $100K–$200K on the table annually
- Missed revenue reduces hospital valuation by $1–2M (at 5–10x earnings)

**Equine-specific (JAVMA 2024 — StableTrack's foundational proof point):**
- Same-day invoices averaged $299
- Next-day invoices averaged $255 (~15% less)
- 1+ day delayed invoices averaged $193 (~36% less)

**Why equine is uniquely vulnerable:**
- Vet is alone at appointment — no front desk, no tech
- Horse owner often not present (trainer/barn manager is contact)
- No connectivity to enter charges in real time
- Documentation happens after hours, sometimes days later
- Common missed items: medications, travel fees, last-minute procedures

Admin burden data:
- 64% of vets report admin workload has doubled (FVE survey 2024–2025)
- Over half of admin work is unpaid
- 30–40% of veterinarians report high burnout levels
- Burnout risk increases significantly past 50 hours/week
