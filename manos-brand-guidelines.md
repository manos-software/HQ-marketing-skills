# Manos Brand Guidelines

> Clarity is scarce. Scarcity is where advantage compounds.

---

## 1. Brand Overview

Manos builds and operates vertical AI infrastructure embedded inside real workflows. The brand speaks to operators, capital allocators, and institutional partners who value structural understanding over trend commentary. Every expression of the Manos identity reflects disciplined execution, economic grounding, and long-term value creation. The visual system is restrained, grounded in natural tones, and designed to signal institutional credibility without corporate sterility.

---

## 2. Logo

### Primary Logo

The Manos logo is a custom lowercase serif wordmark. The letterforms are refined and architectural, with distinctive high-contrast strokes and elegant terminals. The wordmark functions as the sole brand mark — there is no separate icon or logomark at this time (submark variations are TBD).

The logo has two official pairings:

- **Dark Pine wordmark on Linen Grey (#ECEDEA on #1F271B)** — the official Manos logo colorway. This is the default and should be used wherever possible.
- **Charcoal Smoke wordmark on Soft Stone (#191A1E on #F8F6F2)** — the secondary pairing. Use for light-background contexts where a cooler, softer expression is appropriate.

### Logo Variations

- **Official colorway (Dark Pine on Linen Grey)** — default usage across all standard contexts: website headers, documents, presentations, print collateral.
- **Secondary colorway (Charcoal Smoke on Soft Stone)** — use for light-background applications or when the Dark Pine/Linen Grey pairing is unavailable.
- **Submark** — TBD. Until a submark is finalized, use the full wordmark in all contexts. Do not crop or isolate individual letters as a substitute.

### Clear Space

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the cap-height of the letter "m" in the wordmark on all four sides. No text, graphic elements, or container edges should intrude into this zone.

### Minimum Size

- **Digital:** 80px wide minimum
- **Print:** 25mm wide minimum

Below these thresholds, the fine stroke details of the serif letterforms lose legibility.

### Logo Placement Rules

The logo uses two designated pairings. Do not mix wordmark and background colors outside these combinations.

✅ **Official pairing:** Dark Pine wordmark on Linen Grey background — use by default.
✅ **Secondary pairing:** Charcoal Smoke wordmark on Soft Stone background.

Do not place the logo on Sage Stone (#D8DDD4), Slate (#3E4A4F), white, or any background not listed above. The two pairings are intentional — they maintain the brand's tonal warmth and contrast integrity across light and dark contexts.

### Logo Misuse

❌ Don't stretch, compress, or distort the wordmark.
❌ Don't use the logo in any color/background combination other than the two official pairings.
❌ Don't add drop shadows, outlines, glows, or any effects.
❌ Don't rotate or skew the wordmark.
❌ Don't place the logo on busy photographic or textured backgrounds.
❌ Don't set the brand name in a substitute typeface and use it as a logo.
❌ Don't crop or isolate individual letters from the wordmark.
❌ Don't lock up the wordmark with unapproved taglines or descriptors.
❌ Don't place the logo on white, black, or any background outside the designated pairings.

### Neutral Palette

| Swatch | Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|--------|------|-----|-----|-------|
| ⬜ | Linen Grey | #ECEDEA | rgb(236, 237, 234) | Light background. Page fills, content areas, card backgrounds. |

### Color Usage Rules

- **Dark Pine** is the dominant brand color. It should carry the most visual weight in any composition — primary headings, hero backgrounds, key graphic elements.
- **Charcoal Smoke** is a supporting dark. Use it where a cooler, more neutral darkness is needed (footers, secondary panels, data-dense areas). Do not use Dark Pine and Charcoal Smoke at equal weight in the same layout — one should clearly dominate.
- **Sage Stone and Slate** are structural accents. Sage provides breathing room between content sections. Slate provides contrast for secondary text and UI scaffolding.
- **Linen Grey and Soft Stone** are background tones. Soft Stone is warmer and preferred for primary surfaces. Linen Grey is slightly cooler and works well for secondary areas or alternating sections.

✅ **Do:** Pair Dark Pine text on Soft Stone or Linen Grey backgrounds for high readability.
✅ **Do:** Use Slate for body text on light backgrounds when Dark Pine feels too heavy.
❌ **Don't:** Use Sage Stone for text — its contrast ratio against light backgrounds is insufficient.
❌ **Don't:** Combine Dark Pine and Charcoal Smoke as foreground/background — the values are too close.

## 4. Typography

### Primary Typeface

- **Font family:** IBM Plex Sans
- **Source:** Google Fonts / IBM open-source
- **Usage:** Headlines, titles, navigation, UI elements, and structured content. IBM Plex Sans provides the clean geometric structure that anchors the brand's institutional voice.

### Secondary Typeface

- **Font family:** Geist
- **Source:** Vercel (open-source)
- **Usage:** Body copy, long-form content, captions, and supporting text. Geist's slightly warmer proportions balance the precision of IBM Plex Sans and improve readability at smaller sizes.

### Type Scale

| Style | Font | Weight | Size | Line Height | Letter Spacing | Usage |
|-------|------|--------|------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Display | IBM Plex Sans | SemiBold (600) | 56px / 3.5rem | 1.1 | -0.02em | Hero headings, keynote statements |
| H1 | IBM Plex Sans | SemiBold (600) | 40px / 2.5rem | 1.2 | -0.01em | Page titles |
| H2 | IBM Plex Sans | Medium (500) | 28px / 1.75rem | 1.3 | 0 | Section headings |
| H3 | IBM Plex Sans | Medium (500) | 20px / 1.25rem | 1.4 | 0 | Sub-section headings |
| Body | Geist | Regular (400) | 16px / 1rem | 1.6 | 0 | Paragraphs, descriptions, long-form |
| Body Small | Geist | Regular (400) | 14px / 0.875rem | 1.5 | 0 | Secondary body, sidebar text |
| Caption | Geist | Medium (500) | 12px / 0.75rem | 1.4 | 0.02em | Labels, metadata, timestamps |

### Typography Rules

- Limit to IBM Plex Sans and Geist in any single layout. No third typeface.
- Reserve all-caps for short labels, navigation items, or overlines — never for body text or headings longer than three words.
- Minimum body text: 16px digital, 10pt print. Never set Geist below 12px.
- IBM Plex Sans carries the structural weight — use it for anything that names, labels, or organizes. Geist carries the narrative weight — use it for anything that explains or describes.
- When only one typeface is available (e.g., plain-text emails), default to IBM Plex Sans.

## 5. Voice & Tone

### Brand Personality

- **Structural:** We speak from systems-level understanding. Every claim is grounded in how something works, not how it feels.
- **Measured:** We do not chase trends, manufacture urgency, or inflate significance. Authority comes from clarity, not volume.
- **Grounded:** We describe real workflows, real constraints, and real economic outcomes. Specificity is the standard.
- **Disciplined:** Every sentence advances the argument. No filler. No decoration. No hedging for the sake of comfort.

### Voice Principles

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|------------|
| Calm, confident, precise | Breathless, defensive, combative |
| Systems thinkers who analyze workflows and economics | Trend commentators who chase headlines |
| Operators embedded in real industries | Evangelists selling a future that doesn't exist yet |
| Specific — we name verticals, constraints, and proof points | Abstract — we never gesture vaguely at "transformation" |
| Economically grounded — we reason about retention, switching costs, and compounding | Hype-driven — we never claim "revolutionary" or "game-changing" |

### Tone Across Contexts

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Website / Institutional | Measured, forward-looking, execution-oriented | "We build AI infrastructure inside vertical workflows where context compounds over time." |
| LinkedIn / Operator Mode | Direct, grounded, strategic — shows execution in motion | "Deployed the intake automation across three firms last quarter. Processing time dropped 60%." |
| Long-form / Substack | Builder's voice — structural depth, scene-based, diagnostic | "If you walk into most mid-market accounting firms right now, you will see the same architecture failing the same way." |
| Hiring / Fellowship | Disciplined, culturally clear, standards-forward | "We hire operators who think in systems and ship inside constraints." |

### Voice Altitude System

Manos operates three voice altitudes. Substance does not change — only proximity and cadence shift.

**Altitude 1 — Institutional.** The organization speaking formally. Website, press, capital narrative, thesis pieces. Measured, institutional, forward-looking. Structured as context → action → economic implication. Close with inevitability, not intensity.

**Altitude 2 — Operator (Group).** The team speaking as practitioners. LinkedIn company page, product updates, hiring signals. Direct, grounded, disciplined. Shorter sentences, tighter pacing, clear momentum. Still no hype.

**Altitude 3 — Operator (Builder).** An individual thinking aloud with structural depth. Substack, strategic posts, field notes. May use "I" for personal observation, "We" for execution. Scene-based openings, structural diagnosis, system reveals, economic implications.

### Writing Do's and Don'ts

✅ **Do:** Use declarative sentences. State what is, not what might be.
✅ **Do:** Ground every claim in structure, proof, or operational specificity.
✅ **Do:** Frame AI as embedded infrastructure inside workflows.
✅ **Do:** Use short paragraphs with rhythm. Longer sections only when depth requires it.
✅ **Do:** Close pieces calmly. Inevitability, not intensity.

❌ **Don't:** Use buzzwords: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "unprecedented."
❌ **Don't:** Personify AI or frame it as magic.
❌ **Don't:** Use em dashes.
❌ **Don't:** Use "It's not about X, it's about Y" or "This, not that" formulas.
❌ **Don't:** Make unverifiable external claims. If it cannot be measured or structurally explained, do not claim it.
❌ **Don't:** Manufacture urgency or use manipulative emotional triggers.
❌ **Don't:** Adopt VC futurism tone, PE roll-up tone, or influencer-style AI commentary.
